# Client Release

Draft/reference document. Not legal advice.

## Plain Summary

This document lets the Client use delivered edited files for personal use.

Personal use includes personal prints, personal social media, and a personal portfolio. It does not include selling, licensing, advertising, paid content, editorial publication, competition entry, or re-editing the images unless the Photographer gives written permission.

## Delivered Files

The Client may receive selected high-resolution edited digital files from the photography session with the Photographer.

The Photographer owns the copyright to the photographs under applicable copyright law. Delivery of edited image files does not transfer copyright, ownership of the photographs, or permission to use unedited files.

The Photographer is not required to deliver every image created during the session. Unedited files are not normally delivered unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

## Personal Use License

The Photographer grants the Client a worldwide license to reproduce delivered edited images for personal use.

Personal use may include:

- making personal prints
- copying files for personal backup
- emailing or messaging images to friends, family, or collaborators
- posting images on personal social media
- displaying images in a personal portfolio

Personal portfolio may include a personal website, personal social media, and a personal profile on a model, casting, artist, or portfolio platform, as long as the image is used to present the Client as an individual and is not used for paid content, advertising, product promotion, sale, editorial publication, competition entry, or promotion of a third-party brand, agency, service, or platform.

This license has no expiry date unless another written agreement says otherwise.

## Credit

Credit to Ignat Kudriavtsev is appreciated when images are posted, but it is not required.

The Client may request no public name credit where privacy is important.

## Limits on Client Use

The Client may not use the images for commercial purposes, paid content platforms, advertising, product promotion, sale, resale, stock photography, financial gain, competition entry, editorial publication, or promotion of unrelated products or services without written permission from the Photographer.

The Client may not license, sell, transfer, or give the images to another person, brand, publication, agency, company, or platform for their own use without written permission from the Photographer.

The Client may not present the images in a way that falsely suggests the Photographer endorses an unrelated product, service, political cause, organization, or platform.

## Editing and Presentation

The Client may not alter the images by adding filters, re-editing, beauty retouching, AI edits, heavy automatic effects, color changes, removing a watermark where present, or changing the image in a way that may affect authorship, meaning, or presentation.

Ordinary platform resizing, compression, and cropping for personal social media display is allowed if it does not materially change the meaning of the image, misrepresent the work, remove a watermark where present, or create a misleading presentation.

## File Storage

The Client is responsible for keeping their own copies of delivered files.

The Photographer may keep archive copies as long as reasonably needed for artistic, legal, rights-management, backup, archive, or record-keeping purposes, but this release does not require the Photographer to maintain, replace, or re-deliver digital files after delivery.

## Legal Terms

This release is governed by Finnish law unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing. This document replaces earlier discussions about the same subject. Together with any TFP agreement, model release, and written file-delivery terms signed for this collaboration, it is the full agreement between the Parties on this subject. If one part cannot be enforced, the rest remains in effect as far as legally possible. Written approval, written permission, and signatures may be given by physical signature, electronic signature, email, message, or another written method that reasonably identifies the sender and the agreed terms.
